Rizal's Madrid
May 1, 1882 -- Rizal left Calamba and reached Manila after 10 hours via carromata.
Only Paciano, Saturnino, and Uncle Antonio Rivera knew about this trip to avoid detection by Spanish authorities.
Paciano made sure Rizal left the country secretly by securing Rizal's passport with the name Jose Mercado.
Rizal stayed in Manila for two days before his trip to Spain and was scheduled to depart from Manila on May 3rd.
He woke up early, heard mass at Sto. Domingo Church in Intramuros (Walled City) and then went to Pasig River to board the Salvadora, bound for Singapore.
Aboard on the Salvadora, he heard Spanish-speaking travellers, speaking ill of the country.
After one week of sailing, the steamer docked at Singapore and Rizal stayed there for two days, visiting its tourist spots. He spent sightseeing the city including its famed Botanical Garden, Buddhist temples, and the monument of Sir Thomas Standord Raffles, the founder of Singapore.
From Singapore, he boarded the Djemnah, which would ferry him to France.
Djemnah had its stopover at the Point Galle in Ceylon and Port Said at Suez Canal.
June 11, 1882 -- Djemnah reached Naples, whose panoramic beauty and lively people impressed Rizal.
From Naples, the steamer sailed to France and docked at the harbor of Marseilles on June 12, 1882.
From Marseilles, Rizal took the train for Barcelona.
June 15, 1882 -- Rizal reached the entry point to Spain.
June 16, 1882 -- Rizal reached Barcelona, whose residents exhibited open-heartedness, hospitality, and courage.
Rizal was welcomed at Barcelona by Filipinos at Ateneo Municipal.
Rizal's welcome party was held at favorite coffee house at Plaza de Catalu√±a.
One of his missions in Barcelona: make a name for himself in the name of writing.
August 20, 1882 -- El Amor Patrio or Love of Country (written when he was 21 years old) -- first nationalistic essay came out in Diariong Tagalog in Manila under the pseudonym Laong Laan.
He first used the term Tierra Extanjera (foreign land) referring to Spain.
After El Amor Patrio, he stopped producing nationalistic articles.
He enrolled in philosophy and letters and licentiate in medicine at the Universidad Central de Madrid.
He took lessons in painting and sculpture at the Academia de San Fernando.
He took the medical examination from June 5 to 26 and passed, awarding him the degree of Licentiate in Medicine.
He also passed the examination in Greek, Latin, and world history.
He took lessons in fencing at the Schools of Sanz and Carbonell.
He took lessons in English, French, and German in Madrid Ateneo.
He tried to take the examination in Roman law for possible enrolment in Law.
He continued his doctorate degree in medicine but was not conferred the Doctor's diploma due to his failure to pay the fee for his thesis defense.
He also completed his degree of Licentiate in Philosophy and Letters.
For Rizal, there is no difference between the educational system in Madrid and Manila.
It was in Madrid where he completed one half of his novel, Noli Me Tangere.
